The background is the reason the model exists.

A bioengineering degree with a mathematics minor gives you an unusual combination: the biological and clinical vocabulary to understand what an audiogram actually represents, and the statistical foundation to build a model that takes it seriously.

AudiLens was built by someone who spent years building production software systems before turning that foundation toward a problem in healthcare data that hadn't been solved — not because it was technically difficult, but because no one had built the right tool for it.

The technical infrastructure — on-premise deployment, HIPAA-compliant data architecture, outcome logging, and the scoring pipeline — reflects the same engineering discipline applied to the clinical problem.
