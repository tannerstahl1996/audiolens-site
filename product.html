How AudiLens works

From audiogram export to ranked outreach list — powered by a longitudinal progression model trained on 14,553 NHANES audiograms and improving monthly on your own data.

The model

XGBoost with SHAP explainability

We chose XGBoost deliberately over neural networks for three reasons that matter for clinical deployment: it trains effectively on datasets under 15,000 patients without overfitting; it produces per-patient SHAP explanations that translate directly into plain-language reasons your staff can read; and it runs inference on a 1,000-patient panel in under 100ms on commodity hardware with no GPU required.

The model scores every patient aged 45+ who is not currently using hearing aids. Each patient gets a 0–1 risk score and a priority tier — High (top 20%), Medium (20–50%), or Routine.

Four features. Clinically grounded.

age

Age in years

Strongest population-level predictor of PTA elevation. Each additional decade above 55 is associated with substantially higher mean PTA in NHANES data.

PTA

PTA — better ear

4-frequency pure tone average (500Hz, 1kHz, 2kHz, 4kHz) of the better ear. The clinical standard for hearing ability measurement and hearing aid candidacy determination.

HF

High-frequency slope

Mean 4kHz threshold minus mean 500Hz threshold across both ears. The characteristic sloping pattern of age-related sensorineural loss — the earliest audiometric indicator of progressive cochlear damage.

♂♀

Sex

Men have approximately twice the hearing loss prevalence of women in the 20–69 age group per NIDCD data. A consistent predictor in published presbycusis progression models.

Training data

14,553 audiograms. 7 cycles. One nationally representative foundation.

The initial model is trained on publicly available data from the CDC's National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES) — the gold standard for US population health surveillance. Seven cycles spanning 2001–2016, pure-tone air conduction thresholds at 500Hz through 8kHz for both ears, merged with demographic data.

Explainability

Your staff sees reasons. Not scores.

SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) decomposes every patient's score into per-feature contributions. The top three drivers are translated into plain language your front desk can read and act on immediately — no clinical training required.

"High-frequency loss accelerating"

hf_slope feature elevated — 4kHz thresholds significantly worse than 500Hz thresholds. Classic early presbycusis pattern.

"Last visit 22 months ago"

Time since last audiometric evaluation exceeds the 18-month threshold combined with elevated risk score.

"Age-adjusted loss above peer baseline"

PTA tracking materially ahead of age-matched population norm — progressing faster than expected for this patient's age.

All outreach language is administrative — never clinical. AudiLens does not diagnose hearing loss or recommend treatment. All clinical decisions are made by the treating audiologist.

HIPAA & Security

All patient data stays inside your building.

AudiLens is packaged as a Docker Compose deployment that runs on your server or within your own cloud tenant. No PHI ever leaves the practice environment. A Business Associate Agreement (BAA) is executed before any data access.

On-premise deployment

Entire stack runs within your environment. AudiLens never operates or controls the environment in which PHI is processed.

Encryption & audit logging

AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit, append-only audit log retained 6 years per HIPAA requirements.

Aggregate metrics only

De-identified practice-level performance metrics (queue utilization, conversion rate) transmit to AudiLens nightly over encrypted HTTPS. No patient-level data ever transmitted.
