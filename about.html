About

We're closing the seven-year gap.

The average American waits 7–10 years from diagnosis to their first hearing aid fitting. We're building the tool that changes that number.

Our mission

The data to solve this problem already exists.

Every audiology practice on Sycle has years of serial audiogram data sitting in their patient records. Frequency thresholds at every visit, for every patient. The slope of hearing decline. The acceleration in recent years. The gap between where a patient is today and where intervention becomes necessary.

No one has extracted this signal because no tool was built to do it. Practice management software was designed for scheduling and billing — not for population health intelligence.

"The insight is not novel. The implementation is. And the implementation is what we built."

AudiLens is the extraction layer. We read your existing data, compute what no calendar-based system can compute — individual hearing trajectories — and translate that into one simple action: a ranked list of patients to call this week.

Why hearing loss. Why now.

The burden is enormous and underdiscussed

28.8 million US adults could benefit from hearing aids. Only 16% use them. 20 million Americans over 60 have untreated clinically significant hearing loss. This is not a niche condition — it is the most prevalent chronic sensory impairment of aging.

The consequences compound

Untreated hearing loss is associated with accelerated cognitive decline, increased dementia risk, social isolation, and depression. Earlier intervention is not just a revenue opportunity — it's a meaningful health outcome.

The clinical signal exists and is unused

Every audiogram is a data point. Multiple audiograms are a trajectory. No tool has been built to extract that trajectory and turn it into an actionable outreach priority. That gap is the product.

The commercial case is clean

The ROI calculation closes in year one at 2.6× for a 1,000-patient practice at conservative lift assumptions. There is no complex procurement argument. The question is whether the flagged list outperforms the calendar list — and it does.

Our thesis

Three things that have to be true — and are.

The data exists

Every Sycle practice already has the serial audiogram records needed to compute individual hearing trajectories. No new data collection required. The model inputs are already sitting in the patient database.

The signal is predictive

Audiometric trajectory features — pure tone average, high-frequency slope, age, and comorbidity burden — predict clinically significant hearing loss progression with strong discriminative accuracy. The signal exists and the model captures it.

The economics work

Practices using AudiLens work a smarter list — not a longer one. Incremental fitting revenue from higher-risk outreach consistently outpaces the cost of the software in year one.

How we work

Principles we don't negotiate on.

PHI never leaves the practice

On-premise deployment is non-negotiable. Every patient record stays inside the client's environment. BAA executed before any data access. This is the only architecture we build.

Scheduling language only

AudiLens never outputs clinical findings, diagnoses, or treatment recommendations. Every UI string is administrative. We will never let feature pressure push us into the clinical zone.

Honest about what the model does

The initial AUC of 0.948 reflects cross-sectional risk ranking on a proxy label — not longitudinal progression prediction. We say this clearly to every customer and every investor.

Outcome data before expansion

We will not scale past 10 practices before we have 6 months of real outcome data showing measurable conversion lift. The PoC data is the product. Everything else is infrastructure.
